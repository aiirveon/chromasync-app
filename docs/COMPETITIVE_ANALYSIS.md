## Market Context

The story development tools market splits into two groups that serve completely different needs. The first group is professional screenwriting software: Final Draft, Celtx, WriterDuet. These tools format scripts. They do not help you develop the story. They assume you already know what you are writing.

The second group is AI writing assistants: ChatGPT, Sudowrite, Claude, Jasper. These tools generate content for you. They are fast and capable, but they replace the writer's process rather than supporting it. A filmmaker who uses ChatGPT to write their story has a screenplay, but it is not their story.

Ojuit Story Engine sits in neither group. It is a guided story development tool where the AI asks questions and the writer commits answers. The output belongs entirely to the writer because every word in the story bible came from them.

## Competitor Analysis

### Final Draft

**What it is:** The industry standard screenwriting software. Used on every major Hollywood production.

**What it does well:** Formatting, industry acceptance, revision tracking, collaboration for professional teams.

**What it does not do:** Story development. Final Draft is a formatting tool. It has no AI layer, no structural guidance, no character development features. You need to already have your story before Final Draft is useful.

**Why Ojuit wins here:** Final Draft serves professional writers who already know their craft. ChromaSync serves the filmmaker who is still discovering their story. These are different problems entirely.

---

### Celtx

**What it is:** A screenwriting and production planning tool aimed at indie filmmakers and students.

**What it does well:** Affordable, covers pre-production planning, accessible to beginners, includes basic story development templates.

**What it does not do:** AI-guided discovery. Celtx has templates and prompts but no AI layer. Its story development tools are static forms, not responsive conversations. The suggestions are generic and not grounded in the writer's specific idea.

**Why Ojuit wins here:** Every Ojuit suggestion is generated from the writer's own committed answers. Celtx shows the same template to every user. The specificity gap is significant.

---

### ChatGPT and Claude (as writing tools)

**What they are:** General purpose AI assistants that can generate story content on request.

**What they do well:** Speed, flexibility, breadth of knowledge about story structure and screenwriting craft. Can generate a full story outline in seconds.

**What they do not do:** They write the story for you. A writer who asks ChatGPT for a logline gets a logline, but they did not develop it. They accepted it. The writer's agency is removed from the process. There is also no persistence, no structured flow, and no Story Bible accumulating over multiple sessions.

**Why Ojuit wins here:** Ojuit is opinionated about keeping the writer in the process. The AI never writes the story. It asks a question, offers a suggestion if asked, and the writer commits their own answer. The story that emerges belongs to the writer in a way that a ChatGPT output never does.

---

### Sudowrite

**What it is:** An AI writing tool specifically designed for fiction writers. More sophisticated than general AI assistants for creative work.

**What it does well:** High quality prose generation, scene expansion, character voice tools, strong understanding of fiction craft. Genuinely useful for writers who want AI to help with execution.

**What it does not do:** Structured story development from scratch. Sudowrite works best when you already have something written and want to develop it further. It does not guide you from a raw idea through structure. It is also oriented toward prose fiction rather than screenplay structure.

**Why Ojuit wins here:** Ojuit is specifically designed for the blank page problem. Sudowrite is for writers who already have pages. The target stages of the writing process are different.

---

### StudioBinder

**What it is:** A production management platform with some screenwriting features and story development tools.

**What it does well:** End-to-end production management, shot lists, call sheets, scheduling. Strong for small crews managing a production.

**What it does not do:** AI-driven story development. Its writing tools are templates and structured forms without any AI layer.

**Why Ojuit wins here:** Ojuit is a creative development tool. StudioBinder is a production management tool. They serve different phases of the filmmaking process and are not in direct competition.

## Positioning

Ojuit is the only story development tool that combines structured screenwriting methodology (Save the Cat, Truby, Story Circle) with a context-aware AI that grounds every suggestion in the writer's own committed inputs, and persists the entire story state so the writer can return to the same stage across multiple sessions. It also provides the only integrated colour intelligence pipeline for solo indie filmmakers covering pre-shoot, on-shoot, and post correction with LUT export.

The positioning statement: for solo indie filmmakers who have a story they cannot yet see clearly and footage that needs to look like it belongs together, Ojuit is the guided development tool that helps them discover their story and maintain colour continuity across their shoot.

---

## Where Ojuit Is Currently Weak

The Colour and Story products do not yet have a workflow connection. A filmmaker who finishes their story bible cannot automatically carry that story context into their pre-shoot colour planning. That integration is a future product opportunity.

Ojuit has no community or social layer. Tools like Sudowrite and WriterDuet benefit from writer communities that share work, give feedback, and build network effects. Ojuit is currently a solo tool.

The Story product has no export capability in V1. A completed story bible cannot be exported to PDF, sent to a collaborator, or imported into Final Draft. This limits how useful the output is beyond the platform.

The colour correction model is trained on synthetic data. Full bias validation against real diverse footage across skin tones and lighting conditions is deferred to V2.
